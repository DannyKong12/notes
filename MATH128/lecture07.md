#### Converting between polar and cartesian
__Ex1:__ $x^2+y^2=R^2$ => $r=R$  

__Ex2:__ $(x-1)^2+y^2=1$
$$\begin{align}
r = f(\theta) for (r\geq0) \\
(x-1)^2+y^2 &= 1 \\
x^2-2x+1+y^2 &= 1 \\
x^2-2x+y^2 &= 0\\
r^cos^2(\theta)-2rcos(\theta)+r^2sin^2(\theta) &= 0\\
r^2-2r(cos(\theta))&=0 \\
r(r-2cos(\theta))&=0 \\
r = 2cos(\theta)
\end{align}$$

__Ex3:__ Convert $r=\frac{1}{1-sin(\theta)}$
$$\begin{align}
r-r\sin(\theta))&=1 \\
\sqrt{x^2+y^2}-y&=1 \\
\sqrt{x^2+y^2}&=1+y \\
x^2+y^2&=1+2y+y^2 \\
y&=\frac{1}{2}(x^2-1) \\
\end{align}$$
